How do computers obtain routes?

> When looking at the table, you can see a letter at the start of each line, like D or R. These letters tell you how the computer learned about that route.: 

D   10.1.3.0/24 [90/30720] via 10.1.1.4, 00:00:05, GigabitEthernet0/2

-> What does this letter tell us?:

  • L is the ONE IP of your router in your subnet. C is connected, the range of the entire subnet, meaning it just knows it automatically.
  • S means static, this means someone painstakingly manually told the router how to get from A to B, every step along the way
  • D, O, R are all protocols.
  • D is for EIGRP, routers every 5 seconds send "hello packets", these hold info about all routes other routers hold
  • â†³ So, previously unknown routers tell every other router in the neighbourhood how to get to it's destination, now everyone knows.
  • O is for OSPF, they also send hello packets, but require area IDs for your router to accept that information (which just means be close)
  • R is for RIPv2, an older protocol, but also sends hello packets but to everyone every 30 seconds.
