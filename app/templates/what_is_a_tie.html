If two paths tell you how to get somewhere, how do you chose?

> In the table you can see two things that are used to determine which path is better: The first number in brackets, and the second number in brackets. 

    D   10.1.3.0/24 [90/30720], via 10.1.1.4, 00:00:05, GigabitEthernet0/2 [Entry 1]
    O   10.1.3.0/24 [110/20], via 10.1.3.1, 00:00:05, GigabitEthernet0/3 [Entry 2]
    O   10.1.3.0/24 [110/10], via 10.1.2.5 00:00:05, GigabitEthernet0/4 [Entry 3]

-> What are these 2 values, how do they help us determine what path is best?:

  • There are two things to consider when deciding which route to use
  • When comparing different protocols, The first [90/30720], is the administrative distance (AD), highly technical people decided each protocol has a number, the lower the better.
  • ↳ The AD may vary, usually 0 [Connected],1 [Static],90 [EIGRP],11 0[OSPF],120 RIPv2], look at entries 1 and 2

  • When comparing in the same protocol, the second number [90/30720] is the metric, which is a value that tells you how "far" the destination is.
  • ↳ The lower the metric, the better the path. Look at entries 2 and 3
  • Different protocols decide metrics differently, this is why you can not cross compare.
